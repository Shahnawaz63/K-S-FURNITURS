Our Materials

Solid Woods

We use a variety of solid woods, including Oak, Beech, and Acacia. Oak is renowned for its strength and beautiful grain. Beech offers smooth texture, while Acacia provides rich, contrasting patterns. These hardwoods ensure your furniture is durable and timeless.

Rich Veneers

Our Walnut Veneer pieces, like the Mid-Century TV Stand, offer the stunning beauty of exotic woods in a sustainable, stable, and warp-resistant form. Each sheet is carefully selected to ensure a beautiful, consistent grain pattern that elevates any design.

Premium Fabrics

Comfort is key. We select high-quality textiles like plush Velvet and breathable Linen Blends. These fabrics are chosen for their durability, texture, and rich color, ensuring your upholstered pieces are both beautiful and built to last.

Durable Metals

From sleek Steel Frames to elegant Gold-Plated Legs, metal provides the structural integrity and modern accent for many of our pieces. Our metals are treated for longevity, resisting scuffs and wear while providing a clean, contemporary look.

Caring for Your Wood Furniture

  • Dust Regularly: Use a soft, lint-free cloth to remove dust.
  • Wipe Spills Immediately: Use a slightly damp cloth and wipe dry. Do not use excessive water.
  • Use Coasters: Protect surfaces from heat marks and moisture rings by using coasters and placemats.
  • Avoid Direct Sunlight: Prolonged exposure to sunlight can cause fading and damage to the wood finish.

Caring for Your Upholstery

  • Vacuum Regularly: Use a soft brush attachment to remove dust and debris from your sofas and chairs.
  • Blot, Don't Rub: For spills, gently blot the area with a clean, dry cloth. Rubbing can push the stain deeper.
  • Check Care Tags: Always refer to the manufacturer's care tag (often marked 'W', 'S', 'W/S', or 'X') before using any cleaning solutions.
  • Rotate Cushions: Flip and rotate seat and back cushions weekly to ensure even wear.

Caring for Metal Accents

  • Wipe Clean: Use a soft, damp cloth to wipe down metal legs and frames. Dry immediately to prevent water spots.
  • Avoid Harsh Chemicals: Do not use abrasive cleaners, as they can scratch or damage the metal finish.
  • Address Scratches: For painted metal, minor scratches can often be touched up with a matching paint pen.
